Compact to Flagship

Compact / Entry

Audi A3

8P (2006–2013) and 8V (2013–2020). Shared platform with Golf/GTI. The most accessible Audi — and the one with the most options for what's under the hood. Manual vs. S tronic, 1.8T vs. 2.0T, FWD vs. quattro.

Mid-Size Sedan

Audi A4

B7 (2006–2008), B8 (2008–2016), B9 (2017+). The most common Audi we see. B8 2.0T oil consumption and timing chain issues are well-documented and fixable. B9 is a significant reliability improvement.

Sporty Sedan / Coupe

Audi A5 & S5

B8 and B9 generations. A5 shares nearly all drivetrain components with A4 — same service considerations, same known issues. S5 with the 4.2 FSI V8 is a different engine entirely; beautiful, thirsty, and needs timing chain attention.

Full-Size Sedan

Audi A6

C6 (2005–2011) and C7 (2012–2018). Step up to the 3.0T supercharged V6 and the supercharger coupler issue becomes a near-certainty by 100K miles. S6 with the 4.0 TFSI twin-turbo: serious power, demanding maintenance.

The SUV Lineup

Compact SUV

Audi Q5

First-gen (2009–2017) and second-gen (2018+). The first-gen with the 2.0T shares the B8 A4's engine issues. Second-gen EA888 Gen 3 is substantially more reliable. Our most-seen Audi alongside the A4.

Mid-Size / Full-Size SUV

Audi Q7

First-gen 4L (2007–2015) with 3.0 TDI and 3.0T petrol. Air suspension, 3.0T supercharger coupler, and timing chain are the main service items. Second-gen (2017+) on MLB platform: improved but different set of considerations.

Sub-Compact SUV

Audi Q3

First-gen (2012–2018) on the PQ35 platform. 2.0T shared with Golf/A3. Relatively straightforward to service, lower repair costs than the larger Q-series. Known for: timing chain on early 2.0T, DSG service on S tronic variants.
