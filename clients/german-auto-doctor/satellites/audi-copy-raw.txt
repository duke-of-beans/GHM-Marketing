Read Post All Posts → Common Questions Audi Owner FAQ What's the difference between a quattro and an S tronic transmission? Quattro is Audi's all-wheel-drive system — a brand name for the mechanical AWD drivetrain, not a transmission type. S tronic is Audi's dual-clutch automatic transmission (also called DSG in other VAG brands). You can have either or both: an A4 quattro with an S tronic transmission, or a front-wheel-drive A3 with an S tronic. They're independent systems. Quattro refers to how power is distributed to the wheels; S tronic refers to how the engine connects to the drivetrain. How often does an Audi really need an oil change? Audi's LongLife service indicator can push intervals to 10,000 miles or more — which works fine if you're doing highway miles in a newer car with fresh oil. For most Simi Valley driving (more stop-and-go than highway), 5,000 to 7,500 miles is a more conservative and engine-protective interval. On high-performance engines (S4, RS5, etc.), shorter intervals are worth the cost. Always use the oil spec printed on your oil cap or in the owner's manual — not what the shop has on the shelf. Is an extended warranty worth it on a used Audi? It depends heavily on the car's age, mileage, and the specific terms of the warranty. On a high-mileage B8 A4 with a pre-2013 2.0T, an extended warranty that covers engine work (specifically piston rings and timing chain) could pay for itself quickly. On a well-maintained second-gen Q5 under 60K miles, the math is less compelling. The key questions are: what's explicitly excluded, what's the deductible, and does the warranty pay the shop directly or reimburse you. Read the exclusions list before anything else. Can I take my Audi to an independent shop without voiding the warranty? Yes. The Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act prohibits manufacturers from voiding your factory warranty simply because you used an independent shop for service, as long as the work was performed correctly with appropriate parts and fluids. The dealer cannot require that all service be performed at their facility as a condition of warranty coverage. Document your service — receipts, parts specifications, fluid specs used — and keep them. This matters if a warranty claim ever comes up. How do I know if my Audi needs a DSG service? If you can't remember the last time it was done and your car has a DQ250 wet-clutch DSG (most automatic-equipped A4, A5, A6, Q5 with quattro), it probably needs one if you're past 40,000 miles. Symptoms of overdue DSG service include rough low-speed engagement, hesitation pulling away from stops, and a slight shudder in crawling traffic. If you're seeing fault codes (especially P0776–P0778), have it diagnosed before assuming the transmission needs replacing — most of the time it doesn't. What does walnut blasting cost, and how do I know if my Audi needs it? Walnut blasting typically runs $300 to $600 depending on the engine and shop. If you have a GDI (direct injection) engine — which is every 2.0T and 3.0T Audi — and you're past 60,000 miles without having it done, you almost certainly need it. Symptoms are subtle at first: rough cold idle, slight hesitation on acceleration. By the time it's causing misfires or throwing codes, the buildup is significant. It's more economical to clean on a schedule than to wait for symptoms. Ready to book your Audi service in Simi Valley? We've covered the research.
