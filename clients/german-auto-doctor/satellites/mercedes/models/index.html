Mercedes C-Class

W203 (2001–2007), W204 (2008–2014), W205 (2015–2021). The most common Mercedes on Simi Valley roads. SAM unit issues plague the W203, balance shaft concerns hit W204 M272 engines, and the W205 is the most refined generation by a meaningful margin.

Mercedes E-Class

W211 (2003–2009), W212 (2010–2016), W213 (2017+). The W211 carries rear air suspension and front subframe corrosion concerns. W212 introduces M272 balance shaft risk. W213 on the MRA platform is the best E-Class Mercedes has ever built from a reliability standpoint.

How to Use These Guides

Each model guide covers the full generational history of that platform in the US market: chassis codes, engine options, what changed between generations, and the specific failure patterns we see on the lift in Simi Valley. We also include used-buying checklists and the service items that matter most for that particular model.

Mercedes-Benz vehicles depreciate faster than almost any other luxury brand, which creates real opportunity for buyers who know what to look for. A W212 E-Class with a known maintenance history and no deferred service is an exceptional used car at current market prices. The same car with three missed service intervals and undisclosed AIRMATIC issues is a very expensive problem. The difference between those two outcomes is the knowledge in these guides.

If you already own a Mercedes and want to know what service it needs now, the Services section has interval guides by engine and transmission. For specific warning lights or symptoms, the Common Problems section covers the most frequent issues in detail.
