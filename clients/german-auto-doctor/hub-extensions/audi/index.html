Common Questions

AUDI OWNER FAQ

How much does Audi service cost compared to the dealer?

On most services — oil changes, brake jobs, timing chain replacements — we run 30 to 45 percent less than the Audi dealer in Thousand Oaks or Westlake. The parts are identical OEM or OEM-equivalent. The difference is overhead.

Will servicing my Audi here void my warranty?

No. Under the Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act, dealerships cannot void your warranty simply because you had service performed at an independent shop — as long as the work was done properly and the correct fluids and parts were used. We document everything.

My Audi has the oil consumption issue. What does the fix actually involve?

On 2.0 TFSI engines (particularly the EA888 Gen 1 in B8 A4s and Q5s), excessive oil consumption is usually a piston ring issue. The proper fix is a piston ring replacement — typically a 20 to 25 hour job. Some shops offer a "breather modification" as a cheaper workaround. We don't recommend it — it masks the symptom without fixing the engine.

Do you service Audi DSG transmissions?

Yes. DSG service is one of our most common Audi jobs. Fluid and filter changes at 40,000 miles on the DQ250 wet-clutch version. We also handle mechatronic unit issues — the most common DSG failure point. Full DSG service details →

How long does Audi timing chain service take?

On a 2.0T TFSI, budget a full day — typically 7 to 9 hours of labor. On the 3.0T supercharged V6, it's closer to 12 to 14 hours. We call you when the car's apart if we find anything unexpected, before we go further.
